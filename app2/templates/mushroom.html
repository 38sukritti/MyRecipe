Mushroom Risotto

4 servings
Medium

Creamy, flavorful mushroom risotto made with arborio rice, white wine, and a mix of wild mushrooms. This classic Italian dish is perfect for a cozy dinner and pairs wonderfully with a crisp white wine.

Instructions

Prepare the Mushrooms

Clean and slice the mushrooms. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and cook until golden brown (5-7 minutes). Season with salt and set aside.

Sauté Aromatics

In the same pan, heat remaining oil and butter. Add diced onion and cook until translucent (3-4 minutes). Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Toast the Rice

Add arborio rice and stir to coat with oil. Cook for 2 minutes until the rice becomes slightly translucent at the edges.

Deglaze with Wine

Pour in white wine and stir constantly until fully absorbed.

Add Broth Gradually

Add warm broth 1/2 cup at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until each addition is absorbed before adding more (20-25 minutes total).

Finish the Risotto

Stir in cooked mushrooms and parmesan. Season with salt and pepper. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

Recipe Notes

  • Rice Tip: Don't rinse arborio rice—the starch helps create a creamy texture.
  • Broth Temperature: Keep broth warm to maintain even cooking.
  • Vegetarian Option: Use vegetarian parmesan if needed.
  • Storage: Best eaten fresh, but leftovers can be refrigerated for 2 days.
